Pepperoni Pizza

Pepperoni pizza is a classic favorite featuring a crispy crust, rich tomato sauce, melted mozzarella cheese, and savory slices of pepperoni. It is simple, flavorful, and one of the most popular pizza varieties in North America.

Ingredients

  • 1 ball pizza dough
  • 1/2 cup tomato pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 20–25 slices of pepperoni
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Prepare or roll out your pizza dough on a floured surface.
  3. Place the dough on a pizza tray or baking sheet.
  4. Spread an even layer of tomato pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.
  6. Add slices of pepperoni on top of the cheese.
  7. Optional: sprinkle oregano, basil, or chili flakes for extra flavor.
  8. Bake the pizza in the oven for 10–15 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for 3–5 minutes.
  10. Slice and serve.

Fun Fact

Pepperoni is actually an American invention. While it sounds Italian, traditional Italian pizzas usually use salami instead. Pepperoni pizza became popular in the United States in the early 1900s.
