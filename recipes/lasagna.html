Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian pasta dish made with layers of noodles, seasoned meat sauce, creamy ricotta, and melted mozzarella cheese. It is hearty, comforting, and perfect for family dinners or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a pan, cook ground beef over medium heat until browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce to the cooked beef and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, an egg, salt, and pepper.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom of a baking dish.
  7. Add a layer of noodles over the sauce.
  8. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  9. Add shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
  10. Repeat layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella) until ingredients are used.
  11. Finish with a final layer of sauce and mozzarella cheese on top.
  12. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  13. Remove foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  14. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Fun Fact

Lasagna is one of the oldest types of pasta dishes. Its origins date back to Ancient Greece, long before tomatoes were even used in Italian cooking.
