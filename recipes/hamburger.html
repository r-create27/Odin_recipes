Hamburger

Classic Hamburger

A classic hamburger features a juicy seasoned beef patty served inside a soft bun, topped with fresh vegetables and your favorite condiments. It is simple, satisfying, and one of the most popular comfort foods worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese (optional)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices
  • Pickles
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Ketchup and mustard

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill or skillet to medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape into patties.
  3. Season both sides of each patty with salt and black pepper.
  4. Cook the patties for about 3–4 minutes per side, depending on desired doneness.
  5. If using cheese, place a slice on each patty during the last minute of cooking.
  6. Lightly toast the hamburger buns.
  7. Assemble the burger by placing the patty on the bottom bun.
  8. Add lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, and condiments.
  9. Place the top bun on and serve immediately.

Fun Fact

Despite its name, the hamburger was popularized in the United States, but its name comes from Hamburg, Germany, where minced beef dishes were common.
